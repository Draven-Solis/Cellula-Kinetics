Add a Fertilization Mode Toggle to the reproductive anatomy stage.

Toggle Controls

Create two mutually exclusive options:

- No Fertilization (Default)
- Fertilization Mode

Default state must remain No Fertilization so the current menstrual cycle behavior remains unchanged.

---

Fertilization Mode

When Fertilization Mode is enabled:

Step 1: Ovulation

Continue using the existing ovulation animation.

- One mature ovum is released from the active ovary.
- Ovum travels through the fallopian tube.
- Ovum pauses within the ampulla region of the fallopian tube.

---

Step 2: Sperm Migration

Increase sperm activity.

Animate multiple sperm cells traveling along the biological pathway:

Male Reproductive Tract
→ Vagina
→ Cervix
→ Uterus
→ Fallopian Tube

Requirements:

- Many sperm begin the journey.
- Most sperm gradually disappear before reaching the ovum.
- Only one sperm successfully fertilizes the ovum.
- Motion should follow smooth invisible curved paths.
- Existing sperm transport animations should remain functional.

---

Step 3: Microscopic Fertilization Bubble

When a sperm reaches the ovum:

Display a magnified educational inset bubble.

Inside the bubble show:

1. Sperm contacts ovum membrane.
2. Sperm nucleus enters ovum.
3. Male and female pronuclei move toward each other.
4. Pronuclei fuse.

Display label:

"Haploid Male Nucleus (n) + Haploid Female Nucleus (n)"

Then:

"Diploid Zygote Formed (2n)"

---

Step 4: Zygote Formation

After nuclear fusion:

- Ovum transforms into a zygote.
- Zygote briefly glows.
- Show label:

"Zygote (2n)"

---

Step 5: Cleavage Divisions

Animate rapid mitotic divisions.

Sequence:

1 Cell (Zygote)
↓
2 Cells
↓
4 Cells
↓
8 Cells
↓
16 Cells

Each stage should be visually distinct.

Show educational labels:

"Cleavage Division"

"Morula Formation"

---

Step 6: Morula

At the 16-cell stage:

- Display compact ball of cells.
- Label:

"Morula (16-cell stage)"

- Morula slowly moves toward the uterus.

---

Step 7: Implantation Preparation

When the morula reaches the uterus:

- Existing endometrium remains thick.
- Menstruation does NOT occur.
- Endometrial shedding animation is disabled.
- Uterus remains vascularized.

Display label:

"Uterus Prepared for Implantation"

---

Scientific Accuracy Requirements

- Only one sperm fertilizes the ovum.
- Male nucleus and female nucleus must fuse before zygote formation.
- Cleavage occurs by mitosis.
- Morula is represented as approximately 16 cells.
- Fertilization normally occurs in the fallopian tube, not the uterus.
- Menstruation does not occur after successful fertilization.

---

Visual Requirements

- Smooth animations.
- Educational labels.
- Mobile friendly.
- Preserve all existing functionality.
- No graphic or medical imagery.
- Focus on biological education and scientific accuracy.

Goal: allow students to compare two pathways:

No Fertilization:
Ovulation → Endometrial Shedding → New Cycle

Fertilization Mode:
Ovulation → Fertilization → Zygote → Cleavage → Morula → Uterine Arrival
